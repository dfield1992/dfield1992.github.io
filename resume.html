Radiological Emergency Preparedness (REP) - GIS Specialist and REP Planner

Wisconsin Emergency Management - Madison,WI (July 2015 - March 2018)

Employed in the Radiological Emergency Preparedness (REP) Program as a GIS Analyst and a REP planner. As a GIS Analyst, I am responsible for collecting geospatial data, developing federally required planning maps for counties located within 10 miles of a nuclear power plant, engaging with public and private sectors, managing data acquisition projects, incorporating new GIS initiatives, and much more. As a REP planner, I review and develop Wisconsin Emergency Management responses to draft federal program guidance; participate in REP training programs; research and analyze regulatory documents; coordinate incident response plans for nuclear power plants with surrounding counties and much more.

Currently assigned as one of the two GIS Analyst positions in our State Emergency Operations Center (SEOC). In the case of an SEOC activation, I am stationed in our planning section working with GIS. As an emergency unfolds, my main objectives are to create and disseminate geospatial data that can be easily understood in order to help rescue and recovery efforts.

I’ve served as a GIS Analyst in the SEOC about a half a dozen of times. My most recent activation was the severe storm and flooding that took place in July of 2016. During this time the SEOC was activated for two straight work weeks. In order to maintain continues situational awareness, I created a web viewer to serve as the incidents Common Operating Picture (COP). The COP contains several data layers and feature services that helps explain the severity of the incident. Along with the COP, I configured and implemented the Collector App to help assist FEMA with damage assessment. Some of my GIS work for this emergency declaration was featured into Esri’s ArcNews, 2017 Winter Publication, titled “Web Maps and Apps Accelerate Disaster Assessment, Recovery”.
